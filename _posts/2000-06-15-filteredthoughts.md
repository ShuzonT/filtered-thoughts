title: "why one writes"
date: 2000-06-15
---

writing was invented at least three times in three different parts of the world: from sumerian cuneiform invented in mesopotamia (c. 3200 bce) to mandarin script in china (c. 1200 bce) to mayan glyphs in mesoamerica (c. 300 bce). despite script's apparent age, the ability to read and write, as of the 21st century, has eluded the public for the majority of script's existence. globally, widespread literacy only become commonplace around the mid-to-late 20th century (source).
